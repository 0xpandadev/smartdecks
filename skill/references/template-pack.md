# Curated Template Pack

Use a small set of strong templates instead of a large template library.

## Naming Policy

Use professional design-language names in user-facing output. Do not expose source-template names unless the user explicitly asks.

Keep old names only as internal aliases:
- `Pin & Paper` -> `Analog Editorial Collage`
- `BlockFrame` -> `Pastel Neo-Brutalism`
- `Capsule` -> `Soft Modular Neo-Brutalism`
- `Grove` -> `Botanical Editorial Classicism`

## Expressive Design Systems

Use these when generating HTML decks and the user wants expressive visual design.

### Analog Editorial Collage

Alias: Pin & Paper.

Visual traits:
- Paper texture, tactile composition, editorial pacing
- Handcrafted notes, pins, annotations, marginalia
- Warm, literary, qualitative-research tone

Use for:
- Creator essays
- Qualitative findings
- Workshop debriefs
- Founder reflections

Avoid for dense quantitative or institutional decks.

### Pastel Neo-Brutalism

Alias: BlockFrame.

Visual traits:
- Chunky borders, direct blocks, high-contrast structure
- Pastel-neon accents with strong graphic rhythm
- Useful for confident, modern, design-led business decks

Use for:
- Startup/product pitches
- Business model comparisons
- Process and feature grids
- Modern strategy readouts that can be energetic

### Soft Modular Neo-Brutalism

Alias: Capsule.

Visual traits:
- Pill shapes, modular cards, rounded panels, pastel-pop palette
- Friendly tech/product tone
- Works best when cards are mixed with charts, icons, and process diagrams

Use for:
- AI/product explainers
- Creator or product portfolios
- Tech concept decks
- Web-native narrative decks

Avoid using only pill cards across the whole deck. Add charts, diagrams, and iconography.

### Botanical Editorial Classicism

Alias: Grove.

Visual traits:
- Forest green, cream type, classical editorial serif rhythm
- Warm, reflective, literary, grown-up
- Despite the name, do not use literal plant, flower, leaf, vine, botanical, or ornamental illustrations as decoration.
- Use the style as a color/typography/editorial system only. If a nature motif is content-relevant, convert it into a labeled ecosystem map, stakeholder map, or causal diagram.

Use for:
- Sustainability
- Education
- Culture
- Long-term vision
- Calm research narratives

Avoid:
- Decorative flowers, leaves, vines, corner ornaments, or unrelated nature line art.
- Unlabeled circles, badges, or illustrations that do not explain the slide message.

## Consulting / Executive Design Systems

### Executive Analytical Minimalism

Default for business, executive, strategy, research, policy, and quantitative decks.

Visual traits:
- White or very light gray background
- Black/deep navy text
- Thin rules and generous whitespace
- Exhibit-first layouts
- Blue/teal or restrained neutral accents
- Source, notes, page number, and exhibit number in footer
- Direct chart labels and precise callouts

Use for:
- Analysis slides
- Charts
- Market maps
- Scenario comparisons
- Recommendations
- Board-style updates

### Executive Analytical Minimalism Dark

Use sparingly for emphasis.

Visual traits:
- Deep navy background
- White text
- Strong single message
- Minimal charting
- Big numbers or section labels

Use for:
- Title slide
- Section divider
- Big message
- Final recommendation

### Strategic Report Neo-Corporate

Inspired by recent public strategy-report visual language, abstracted into an original template.

Visual traits:
- Strong green accent
- Bold section labels
- Dense but organized executive summary pages
- A/B/C circular labels
- Comparison grids
- Policy/strategy maps
- Bottom-line implication bands

Use for:
- AI strategy
- Transformation
- Policy report
- Cross-country or cross-option comparisons
- High-conviction strategic argument

### Vector Consulting Linework

Default for MBB-level business diagrams and AI/workflow/value-chain visuals.

Visual traits:
- White background
- Thin black outline illustration
- Limited palette
- Neon pistachio green accent
- Simple characters only when they clarify work, decision, or system flow
- Labels, arrows, steps, and causal structure

Use for:
- AI workflow
- Operating model
- Value creation loop
- Human-in-the-loop system
- Before/after transformation
- Skill factory / capability factory

## Visual Mix Requirement

For a 6-10 slide deck, target this mix unless the content says otherwise:
- 1 title or thesis slide
- 2-3 chart/data/table slides
- 1-2 consulting diagram slides
- 1 comparison/matrix/scorecard slide
- 1 roadmap or implications slide
- 1 closing decision/next-action slide

Cards alone are not enough. Cards are useful for summaries, but the deck must include exhibits that make the argument visible.

## Naming and Safety

Do not name templates after consulting firms in generated artifacts. Use template names above. Do not copy logos, exact pages, proprietary marks, or distinctive firm trade dress.
